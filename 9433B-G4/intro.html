What is Inclusive Education?

Inclusive Education means that all students with or without disablities are learning together and they are always welcomed to join the school. It is given to them to feel they are not different and not feel out of place, it is giving them the life what they want or to live a normal life. Inclusive education also means how the management or staff can handle or teach the students even if they have with or disabilities, they must make the students participate despite their disabilities.

History of Inclusive Education

In 1950, the children with disabilities have the right to receive an education but that time many people believed that they should not learn so it led to the children to go to private education. In 1955, the parents of the children created an organization to gain the right of their children to have education with students who don't have disabilities. Throughout the years, the government realized that the students must have the right to be educated the equally with the students without disabilities, they funded the schools and build also schools who offers inclusive education.

Benefits of Inclusive Education for students with Disabilities

-Parents vision of their child will come true because they will learn or be accepted with or without disabilites

-They will develop social skills and friendships

-Develop their skill in learning

-They will not feel out of place

-Greater opportunities in life

Benefits of Inclusive Education for students without Disabilities

-Respect for all people despite their disablities

-Understanding the rights of all people

-Will learn a meaningful lesson in life

-Learn to accept the differences of people

-Develop the skill of teaching and learning

Common Misconceptions About Inclusive Education

-Seperate is better

Children who are learning together even if they have disabilities because they learn things or values about the life of children.

-Children must be ready to included

The child may not meet the requirements or act like normal to be accepted in the general education

-Parents don't support inclusive education

All parents support inclusive education because they want to be their child to feel normal and not make them feel out of place.
